What Is Workplace Learning?

Learning designed around real work, not isolated training.

Workplace learning connects business goals with work redesign, performance support, coaching, and measurable employee capability building. For enterprises navigating AI, it helps teams adopt new tools while changing workflows, roles, and decision habits.

NACE And LEA Grants

Choose the right workplace transformation pathway before committing resources.

NACE Workplace Learning Consultancy

NACE centres support enterprises with outcome-focused consultancy for workplace learning and employee-driven transformation projects.

  • Useful when you need a structured project to address a business performance gap.
  • Typically involves an enterprise project champion, leaders, team members, and a consultant.
  • Can focus on productivity, digitalisation, automation, and employee professionalisation.

Learning Enterprise Alliance (LEA)

LEA is for enterprises that want to move beyond one-off training and build a learning enterprise: learning is embedded into daily work, employees help co-create improvements, and work redesign is tied to business performance.

  • Helps diagnose whether performance gaps come from skills, workflow, leadership, systems, or alignment issues.
  • Supports people-driven innovation by involving employees in improving products, services, processes, and customer outcomes.
  • Builds internal capability to innovate, redesign work, and deploy new skills continuously after the project.
  • Grant support depends on current eligibility criteria, project scope, and approving agency terms.

Funding & LEA Direction

Plan with grant terms and business outcomes in view.

Public IAL NACE@IAL consultancy information states that eligible SMEs may receive up to 90% funding, while non-SMEs may receive up to 70%; terms and eligibility apply.

IAL's 2026 LEA Awards update frames LEA around people-driven innovation, integrated work-and-learning solutions, and moving learning from activity to business performance.
